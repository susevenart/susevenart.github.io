ABOUT

Su is a London-based artist whose practice moves between sculpture, installation, painting and text. With a background in finance, she examines the invisible systems that shape contemporary life—economic flows, algorithmic structures, attention, and the perception of value.

Her work often begins with rational diagrams, market logic or technological metaphors, but gradually departs from them, entering a more speculative, psychological space. Through restrained forms, modular structures and quiet colour fields, she attempts to visualise how external systems leak into the body: how they reorganise memory, intimacy and the sense of self.

Rather than representing data or illustrating theories, her practice seeks a subtler transformation—turning abstraction into something experiential, and translating systemic tension into material presence. The works are less about explanation and more about the conditions that make a certain image, gesture or structure possible.

Originally trained in economics and finance, she brings analytical thinking into the studio while resisting its limits. This dual grounding allows her to move fluidly between conceptual research and embodied making, producing works that sit between clarity and ambiguity, structure and emotion.
